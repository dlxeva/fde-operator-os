# Synthetic Input Notes

## Scenario

An operations support team manages exception reports across three regional teams. Coordinators believe submission quality is inconsistent, repeat exceptions are rediscovered during follow-up, and closure evidence is weak.

## What The Team Says

- Frontline staff submit screenshots and notes through a web form.
- Coordinators review the submissions and decide whether to open a resolution task.
- Response owners are expected to resolve the issue and upload completion evidence.
- Monthly summaries are assembled manually from spreadsheets and chat history.

## What Actually Seems To Happen

- Staff use unofficial chat groups to ask whether an issue is "serious enough" before formal submission.
- Some low-confidence exceptions are never entered into the formal system.
- Response owners often reply with text-only confirmations instead of evidence.
- Coordinators keep a private spreadsheet to track overdue items because the official system does not flag them reliably.

## Candidate Pain Points

- inconsistent exception qualification
- slow handoff into resolution work
- weak proof of closure
- manual reporting overhead

## Constraints

- no full platform rebuild in phase one
- existing tasking tool must remain the source of truth for resolution tasks
- operators need a visible human review step before any task is created automatically
