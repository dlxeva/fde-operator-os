# Synthetic Input Notes

## Scenario

A property operations team manages safety inspections across three mixed-use buildings. Supervisors believe inspection quality is inconsistent, hazards are often rediscovered during follow-up checks, and closure evidence is weak.

## What The Team Says

- Inspectors submit photos through a mobile form.
- Supervisors review the photos and decide whether to open a remediation ticket.
- Facilities teams are expected to fix the issue and upload completion evidence.
- Monthly reports are assembled manually from spreadsheets and chat history.

## What Actually Seems To Happen

- Inspectors use unofficial chat groups to ask whether a finding is "serious enough" before submitting it.
- Some low-confidence hazards are never entered into the formal system.
- Facilities teams often reply with text-only confirmations instead of photo evidence.
- Supervisors keep a private spreadsheet to track overdue items because the official system does not flag them reliably.

## Candidate Pain Points

- inconsistent hazard qualification
- slow remediation handoff
- weak proof of closure
- manual reporting overhead

## Constraints

- no full platform rebuild in phase one
- existing ticketing tool must remain the source of truth for remediation tasks
- operators need a visible human review step before any ticket is created automatically
