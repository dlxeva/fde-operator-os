# Operational Reality Map

## Actors

- Inspector
- Safety supervisor
- Facilities responder
- Property manager

## Responsibility Chain

- Detect: Inspector
- Decide: Safety supervisor
- Act: Facilities responder
- Approve: Safety supervisor
- Audit: Property manager / monthly review owner

## Current Workflow

1. Inspector captures a photo and optional note during a site walk.
2. If the issue looks ambiguous, the inspector asks in a chat group before formal submission.
3. Supervisor reviews the submission and decides whether it qualifies as a remediation-worthy hazard.
4. Supervisor manually creates a ticket in the facilities system.
5. Facilities responder marks the item as fixed and may attach text or a photo.
6. Supervisor decides whether closure evidence is sufficient.
7. Overdue items are tracked in a private spreadsheet.
8. Monthly summaries are compiled manually.

## Systems / Channels

- mobile inspection form
- unofficial chat group
- facilities ticketing system
- supervisor spreadsheet

## Evidence Artifacts

- SOP: inspection checklist and escalation guide
- Form / spreadsheet: mobile form output, overdue tracker
- Screenshot / photo: hazard photos, closure photos
- Ticket / log: facilities tickets and timestamps

## Exception Paths

- low-confidence findings are discussed informally and may never enter the formal system
- closure may be accepted based on text only when photo evidence is missing

## Failure Points

- qualification threshold is inconsistent across reviewers
- remediation evidence quality is not enforced
- overdue tracking lives outside the source-of-truth workflow
